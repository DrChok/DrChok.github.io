cis-cyclohexene trans-cyclohexene
As the double bond C atoms are sp² hybridised, the molecule should be planar about the C=C. This is readily achieved in cis-cyclohexene. However, in the case of trans-cyclohexene, this will require the remaining 3 CH₂–CH₂–CH₂–CH₂ bonds to be longer (hence weaker) than normal C–C bonds, and the bond angles also deviate significantly from the ideal tetrahedral angle of 109.5º.
